5. Likely reviewer attack points

- [likely] “Remarkable accuracy” and “extremely coarse” require exact metrics, baselines, and uncertainty.
- [likely] Visual similarity is insufficient for turbulence; reviewers would ask for spectra/statistics to detect hallucinated small scales.
- [likely] If train/test snapshots are temporally adjacent, leakage could inflate performance.
- [likely] Two-dimensional cylinder wake and homogeneous turbulence do not establish performance for 3D, wall-bounded, experimental, noisy, or geometry-varying flows.
- [likely] A 50-snapshot claim may depend strongly on stationarity and synthetic downsampling.
- [likely] CNN vs DSC/MS conclusions need ablations showing which multiscale/skip components matter.
- [unknown] Reproducibility risk: code, data, solver setup, architecture, and training protocol are not recoverable from the abstract.
